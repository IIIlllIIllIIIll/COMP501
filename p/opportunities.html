Technology Opportunities

Open-source software creates many opportunities for developing countries, such as lowering costs and improving access; Free software is not necessarily free of charge; it can be sold. However, buyers can resell or distribute it for free, leading to a likely price of zero. While the software itself might be free, there are minimal costs for distribution, such as CDs, printed manuals, or internet communication. These costs are incurred only once, and the marginal cost of redistributing the software is zero, after choosing free software for its low cost, other benefits become clear, such as the ability to adapt it to local needs. Unlike proprietary software companies, which are typically global and concentrated in a few regions, free software can be tailored more easily to specific local requirements.

(THE OPPORTUNITIES OF FREE/LIBRE/OPEN-SOURCE SOFTWARE FOR DEVELOPING COUNTRIES DRAFT, n.d.) Global companies focus on investments with global returns and may neglect local needs due to the natural monopolies created by network effects and proprietary standards. The 2002 FLOSS study revealed that the primary motivation for developers to engage in open-source communities is to learn new skills for free. These skills, valuable for job opportunities and sustaining small businesses, go beyond just using free software. They include programming, understanding copyright law and licenses, and teamwork and team management. Managing large, dispersed teams in open-source projects requires more intensive and subtle coordination than managing smaller, co-located teams in a typical software company.
